# SkillAgents AI Employee Handbook

Last updated: March 2026

This handbook applies to full-time and part-time employees of SkillAgents AI. It covers the internal operating norms that staff members follow at the company. If you are a student or a customer looking for information about enrollment, billing, or the course itself, this is not the right document. Check the product guide or the billing FAQ instead.

## Office locations and remote work bands

SkillAgents AI has two offices. The headquarters is in Bangalore, on Brigade Road, and houses the engineering, product, and operations teams. The satellite office is in San Francisco, on Market Street, and houses the instructor and partnerships teams. Most staff work hybrid, three days in the office and two days from home, with Monday and Friday as the required in-office days. Fully remote roles are reserved for staff who live more than one hundred kilometers from either office. All employees, regardless of location, must have a four-hour daily overlap with Bangalore office hours, which run from ten in the morning to seven in the evening India Standard Time. Team leads publish their core hours on the shared calendar so that collaborators in every time zone know when to schedule sync time.

## Hiring loop and interview rubrics

Every role at SkillAgents AI goes through a five-step hiring loop. The first step is a thirty-minute screening call with the recruiting partner. The second step is a take-home assignment specific to the role. Engineers build a small feature on a public API. Product managers write a short memo on a product decision. Instructors record a ten-minute lesson on a topic of their choice. The third step is a panel with three staff members who evaluate the take-home against a written rubric scored one through five. The fourth step is a values interview with a member of the leadership team. The fifth step is a reference check. Every candidate receives written feedback within one working week of their final interview, regardless of outcome. The hiring bar is set by the calibration committee and is reviewed every six months to make sure it is still catching the right candidates and not drifting in either direction over time.

## Equipment and software allowance

Every employee receives a company laptop on day one. Engineering and product staff get a sixteen-inch MacBook Pro. Instructors and operations staff get a fourteen-inch MacBook Air. Laptops are replaced every three years. If a device fails before the three-year mark, the operations team ships a replacement within two working days. Every employee also receives a one-time four hundred dollar monitor stipend when they join the company, plus a monthly seventy-five dollar software allowance that can be spent on any tool that helps with daily work, such as a writing app, a note-taking tool, or a design program. Receipts are submitted through the expense portal by the fifteenth of the following month and reviewed by the operations team within five working days.

## Travel policy for instructors and staff

Instructors traveling for in-person teaching events follow a simple per diem policy. Domestic travel inside India has a daily per diem of two thousand five hundred rupees for meals and local transport. International travel has a daily per diem of ninety dollars for meals and local transport. Hotels are booked by the operations team with a nightly cap of eight thousand rupees domestic or two hundred twenty dollars international. Flights are booked in economy for trips under six hours of flight time and in premium economy for longer legs. Car rentals must be approved in advance by the instructor lead. Client dinners and team dinners on the road are reimbursed in full with itemized receipts. Personal items such as laundry, movies, minibar food, and spa services are not reimbursable. The expense report for each trip is due within one working week of return. Late submissions delay the next monthly payroll cycle by one full cycle, which is a hard rule and not a suggestion.

## Speaking at external events

Staff members are encouraged to speak at industry conferences, meetups, and podcasts. The process starts by filing a speaking proposal in the internal tracker with the event name, date, proposed topic, and whether the talk will be recorded. The communications team reviews the proposal within three working days and signals approval or asks for edits. Approval rests on two criteria. First, does the topic align with the company messaging themes for the quarter. Second, does the proposed abstract avoid sharing any information that is subject to a confidentiality agreement with a partner or student. Once approved, the speaker works with the communications team on slide review and rehearsal. Two rehearsal sessions are standard, one with the communications lead and one with a peer reviewer from the same team. Staff members may accept honoraria for paid speaking engagements, and those honoraria are kept by the individual staff member, not the company, as long as the event is approved in advance through the process above.

## Internal communication norms

SkillAgents AI runs its internal communication on Slack with a small set of rules that keep the volume manageable. Public channels are default. Private channels are only used for topics that involve personnel, legal, or partner confidentiality. The response time expectation on public channels is one working day. The response time on direct messages is four hours inside working hours. No meetings are scheduled on Wednesdays so everyone has one full day of focused work time each week. Meetings must have an agenda attached to the calendar invite, and meetings without an agenda can be declined with no social cost. Every meeting produces written notes in the shared notes space within twenty-four hours of the meeting ending.

## Performance review cadence

Performance reviews happen twice a year at SkillAgents AI, in April and October. The cadence is written first, then discussed in a live conversation. Every employee writes a self-review two weeks before the review window, covering what they shipped, what they learned, where they need help, and one specific area they want to invest in for the next six months. Their manager writes a parallel review covering the same areas, plus a calibration score on a five-point scale. Both documents are shared with the employee before the review conversation, so there are no surprise ratings on the day of the meeting. The calibration committee meets once at the end of each review window to normalize scores across teams and to catch any managers who have drifted too high or too low relative to the rest of the company. Employees can appeal their review outcome in writing within two weeks of the conversation, and appeals are read by a second manager in a different function from the one who wrote the original review.

## Parental leave and caregiving leave

Primary caregivers at SkillAgents AI receive eighteen weeks of fully paid parental leave for the birth or adoption of a child. Secondary caregivers receive six weeks of fully paid leave on the same terms. The leave must begin within twelve months of the birth or adoption date and can be taken as two separate blocks if the manager approves the split in advance. Health insurance, equity vesting, and performance review eligibility continue uninterrupted for the full duration of the leave. Employees on parental leave are not expected to respond to Slack messages or emails, and their calendar is automatically marked as out of office for the entire period until they return to active duty.

## Sabbatical eligibility after four years

Every staff member who completes four continuous years of service at SkillAgents AI becomes eligible for a one-month paid sabbatical. The sabbatical must be planned at least six months in advance so the team can redistribute responsibilities without a gap. Sabbaticals are not accrued, which means a staff member cannot bank multiple years of unused sabbatical and take a two-month break after eight years. Each four-year anniversary resets the clock and creates a new one-month eligibility window. Sabbaticals cannot be taken during the two weeks before or after a quarterly company all hands, because those are the highest-intensity operational windows of the year and the team needs everyone present.

## Off-boarding checklist for departing staff

When a staff member decides to leave SkillAgents AI, the final two weeks follow a standard knowledge transfer checklist. The departing employee meets with their manager on day one of the final two weeks to list the top five open projects and who will inherit each one. By the end of the first week, every open project has a written handoff document in the shared notes space, covering context, next steps, and key contacts. The departing employee returns their company laptop, any loaned monitors, and any office badges on their final working day, following the equipment return process run by the operations team. After the final day, every former staff member receives an invitation to join the alumni Slack workspace, where they stay in touch with the team and hear about reunions and events.
